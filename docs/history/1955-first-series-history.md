# 1955 Chevrolet First Series Trucks

## The Final Year of the Advance Design Era

---

## Introduction

The 1955 Chevrolet First Series trucks represent the **final year of the legendary Advance Design generation** that began in 1947. These trucks, produced from late 1954 through early 1955, embody eight years of continuous refinement and represent the pinnacle of this iconic design.

When equipped with NAPCO 4-wheel drive, these trucks became versatile workhorses capable of handling the most demanding conditions while maintaining the proven reliability that made Advance Design trucks famous.

---

## The Advance Design Legacy (1947-1955)

### A Revolutionary Beginning

The Advance Design trucks were introduced for the 1947 model year as Chevrolet's first all-new post-war design. Key innovations included:

- **Cab-forward design** - Driver positioned over front axle for better visibility
- **All-steel cab** - Safer and more durable than previous designs
- **Wider cab** - More comfortable for three-across seating
- **Improved visibility** - Larger windshield and windows
- **Smoother styling** - Streamlined, modern appearance

### Evolution Through the Years

| Year | Key Changes |
|------|-------------|
| **1947** | All-new Advance Design introduced |
| **1948-1949** | Minor refinements |
| **1950** | Vent windows added, push-button door handles |
| **1951** | New dashboard design |
| **1952** | Revised grille |
| **1953** | Minor refinements |
| **1954** | One-piece curved windshield (all models), new grille, automatic transmission |
| **1955 1st Series** | Final year, running changes, NAPCO 4x4 popular |

## 1955 First Series Specifics

### What Makes 1955 First Series Unique

The 1955 First Series trucks are distinguished by:

1. **Final Advance Design year** - Last trucks of this generation
2. **Most refined version** - Eight years of improvements
3. **Transitional production** - Built alongside early Second Series
4. **NAPCO availability** - Second year of dealer-installed 4x4 conversions

### Identifying a 1955 First Series

| Feature | 1955 First Series | 1955 Second Series |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Windshield** | One-piece curved glass | Wraparound one-piece |
| **Hood** | Alligator style (front-hinged) | Rear-hinged |
| **Grille** | Five horizontal bars | Egg-crate pattern |
| **Dashboard** | 1954 style carryover | All-new design |
| **Vent Windows** | Present | Present |
| **Cab Corner Shape** | Rounded | Squared |
| **Door Handles** | Push-button | Pull-out |
| **Serial Number Prefix** | Varies by series | H, J, K, L prefix |

### Production Period

- **Start:** Late 1954 (for 1955 model year)
- **End:** Approximately March 1955
- **Overlap:** Some First Series trucks produced alongside early Second Series

### Serial Number Identification

1955 First Series serial numbers follow the same format as 1954:

```

                  S = St. Louis
                  T = Tarrytown
```

## 1955 First Series with NAPCO

### NAPCO Introduction

NAPCO (Northwestern Auto Parts Company) began offering 4-wheel drive conversions for Chevrolet trucks in 1954, making 1955 First Series trucks among the earliest Chevrolet applications.

### Why First Series NAPCO Trucks Are Rare

| Factor | Impact on Rarity |
|--------|------------------|
| Short production window | Limited time to order/install NAPCO |
| Second Series anticipation | Some buyers waited for new design |
| Higher cost | NAPCO added ~$750 to vehicle price |
| Regional demand | Most sold in rural/mountain areas |
| Survival rate | Work trucks often used until worn out |

### First Series NAPCO Advantages

The Advance Design platform offered some advantages for NAPCO conversion:

- **Proven chassis** - Eight years of refinement
- **Simple mechanicals** - Easier installation
- **Rugged construction** - Built for work
- **Strong frame** - Handled 4x4 stresses well
- **Available 4-speed** - Good for off-road use

### Grille Design

The 1955 First Series uses the same grille introduced in 1954:
- Five horizontal bars
- Chrome center bar (Deluxe)
- Painted bars (Standard)
- "CHEVROLET" lettering on hood

### Hood

The "alligator" hood is a defining feature:
- Hinges at front, opens from rear
- Provides excellent engine access
- Side louvers for ventilation

## Transition to Second Series

### The Changeover

In March 1955, Chevrolet introduced the Second Series "Task Force" trucks:
- All-new styling
- Wraparound windshield
- First V8 option in Chevrolet trucks
- Modern dashboard
- New model designations

### Impact on First Series

- First Series production ceased
- Some dealers had both in stock briefly
- First Series prices reduced for clearance
- Many NAPCO installations continued through transition

## Collectibility Today

### Why Collectors Value 1955 First Series

| Factor | Collector Appeal |
|--------|------------------|
| **Final year** | End of an era appeal |
| **Proven design** | Eight years of refinement |
| **Simpler mechanics** | Easier restoration |
| **Strong parts availability** | 1947-55 parts interchangeable |
| **NAPCO rarity** | Extremely collectible with 4x4 |
| **Classic styling** | Iconic Advance Design look |

### Condition Considerations

After 70+ years, condition varies widely:
- Many were worked hard
- Rust is common in certain climates
- NAPCO components often worn or replaced
- Documentation often lost
- Finding complete, original examples is rare

### Values

1955 First Series NAPCO trucks command premiums:
- Original NAPCO equipment adds significant value
- Documentation (original invoice, service records) valuable
- Condition is primary value driver
- Matching numbers highly desirable

## Preservation Priority

### Why Original Matters

For 1955 First Series NAPCO trucks:

1. **Documentation** - Preserve any original paperwork
2. **NAPCO Components** - Keep original transfer case, axle tags
3. **VIN/Serial Numbers** - Document all identification
4. **Period Accessories** - Retain any dealer-installed items
5. **Patina** - Some collectors prefer original wear
6. **History** - Research and document ownership chain

## Conclusion

The 1955 Chevrolet First Series represents the culmination of the Advance Design era—eight years of continuous improvement resulting in the most refined version of this iconic design. When equipped with NAPCO 4-wheel drive, these trucks gained capabilities that made them invaluable for demanding work conditions.

Today, surviving 1955 First Series NAPCO trucks are exceptionally rare and highly prized by collectors who appreciate both the classic Advance Design styling and the engineering significance of early dealer-installed 4-wheel drive conversions. Note that factory-installed NAPCO did not become available until 1956 for GMC and 1957 for Chevrolet (RPO 690), making all 1955 First Series NAPCO trucks dealer or aftermarket conversions.

Your 1955 First Series NAPCO truck is a genuine piece of American automotive history.
